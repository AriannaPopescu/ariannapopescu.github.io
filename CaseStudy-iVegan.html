Project Overview

Description

iVegan is the largest Italian distributor of vegan food, delivering throughout the country quickly and efficiently thanks to the option to purchase online or by visiting the store in Rome. The company is committed to offering a wide selection of products, organic and cruelty-free, prioritizing quality by selecting from the best artisanal businesses and large companies specialized in the sector.

My role

User and Market Research, Wireframing, UI Design, User Testing.

Why I started this project

Problems

Solutions

  • Make the buying process easier.
  • Desing a modern website.
  • Desing an intutive and clear interface.
  • Provide client reviews.

Design process and Timeline

Research

Heuristic Analysis

To determine whether the current desktop and mobile versions of the website comply with usability and functionality criteria, an analysis was conducted using Nielsen's 10 heuristics. This allowed me to identify the strengths and weaknesses of the iVegan website.

Usability

  • Learnability: At first glance, the user is able to perform the main tasks (e.g. purchase), but not all categories are immediately visible.
  • Efficiency: The user is able to learn the site's navigation, although not always in a smooth manner (e.g. some categories are not immediately identifiable and there is no flexibility in filtering products).
  • Memorability:The user is able to use the site with the same ability even after a period of time.
  • Errors: The user is generally able to avoid errors. Some actions, if done by mistake, cannot be undone (e.g. deleting a product from the cart).
  • Satisfaction: The design is not minimalist and not in line with current trends. The content is overly compact and does not allow the page to breathe.

Information Architecture

Competitive Analysis

To determine iVegan's strengths and weaknesses compared to other companies operating in the same market, a comparative analysis was carried out with 5 of the main vegan stores.

The direct competitors of iVegan are Italian e-commerce companies that sell vegan and organic products, both food and non-food.

⟹Analysis Results

From the analysis emerged the differences between iVegan's website and those of the analyzed competitors.

Strengths: presence of a live chat, indication of product expiration date, display of best-selling products.

Weaknesses: lack of FAQ and reviews, limited filtering options, no link to social media profiles, fewer payment options compared to most competitors.

User Research

iVegan's target audience was identified by observing reviews on the web and analyzing interactions on social media profiles (Facebook and Instagram).

From research on the web and social media, a mixed target audience emerged, consisting mainly of women, aged between 25 and 35 years old, with a medium to medium-high income range.

To verify the accuracy of the research and obtain additional insights into the lifestyle and purchasing habits of the target audience, a 16-question survey was administered to a group of people who follow a vegan lifestyle. The survey collected 49 responses, obtained from friends, relatives, and users on social media groups dedicated to the vegan lifestyle.

⟹Survey Results

57% of the respondents to the questionnaire are women, aged between 25 and 34. The users are mostly workers (59%). 47% follow an exclusively vegan diet and mainly purchase food products, prioritizing the quality/price ratio. The majority of users make purchases both in-store and online (48%), with 28% preferring online over physical stores, and using both PC and mobile devices to make purchases (42%). For users, the presence of filters and FAQs on a website is very important, followed by recently viewed products, live chat, and the Compare Products function.

Wireframing

Wireframes Mobile and desktop

I created wireframes for 5 pages.

To ensure the best functionality of the website i designed the wireframes Mobile-first.

To create a consinstent design I started with low-fidelity wireframes drawn on paper, that I, then, recreated digitally in High-fidelity with Figma, following a Grid and a typographic scale, which allowed me to reorganize the elements present on the website and improve their hierarchy, making the website look more minimalistic.

UI Design

Final Design

Firstly, I created a design system that contains information about the grids used, the spacing between elements, typography size and weight, icons and the different button and forms in various states. Starting from the logo design, I identified the colors to be used, analyzing their contrast, to ensure good accessibility and readability.

Then, I moved on to designing the pages and the way in which the user will interact with them and the elements present, creating an interface with a minimal appearance and easy-to-understand functionality.

User Testing

Usability Testing

I will conduct a moderated usability test. Thanks to this type of test, I will be able to obtain both quantitative and qualitative data by observing participants' behavior during interactions with the design.

Quantitative data will be based on the success rate of the task completion.

Qualitative data will be optained from user observations and behavior.

Objectives:

  • Determine if the user is able to navigate the website and complete purchases and actions easily.
  • Identify Pain points and areas of confusion for the user.
  • Optain feedback regarding the design.

Test procedure:

The test will be conducted remotely via Google Meet.

As the sole facilitator, the test will be recorded (using OBS) and analyzed later.

A pilot test will be conducted before the actual test.

Version analyzed:

Desktop, as it is easier to obtain feedback on design and layout thanks to the larger screen size, making it faster to catch any potential errors or issues.

Partecipants:

To ensure the collection of sufficient data, 6 participants will take the test, selected based on target research conducted in the "Research" phase.

The test will be conducted by people who are not familiar with iVegan, including 3 with more experience in using technology devices, the web, and that frequently purchase online, and 3 with less experience, to ensure that the design and its features are minimal, easy to use, and intuitive for different types of people.

Key words:

Simple - Easy - Intuitive - Clear - Organized - Consistent Look - Appropriate Colors

How did you find using the website?

Key words:

Easy - Simple - Minimal - Intuitive - Understandable - Functional

Task 1: You want to purchase a veggie burger, what do you do?

Quantitative data: All participants, both experts and non-experts, were able to find and add the product to the cart easily.

Qualitative data: None of the participants reported any areas of confusion. Less experienced participants appreciated the ability to access categories in different ways (search, navbar, section on the homepage).

Task 2: Since you will have it delivered to your house, you are interested in knowing if the burger will be delivered frozen or not. Where do you click?

Quantitative data: All the expert participants directly reached the FAQ page by going straight to the footer, while 2 of the less experienced ones completed the task indirectly, as they saw the presence of the live chat and clicked on its icon. Inside the live chat, before sending a message, they were redirected through the copy to the FAQ page.

Qualitative data: One of the non-expert participants became confused by the copy 'Phone Orders/Assistance,' focusing on that section and wondering if they should call the phone number provided to request information.

Task 3: You are ready to finalize the purchase, what would you do?

Quantitative data: All participants, both experts and non-experts, were able to complete the task by clicking on the cart icon and accessing the checkout page.

Qualitative data: The non-expert participants, while completing the fields required to finalize the order, paused at the discount code field wondering whether they should enter a code.

⟹Upgrades

  • Change the copy from "Ordini telefonici/Assistenza" to "Ordini telefonici" to avoid confusion and prevent users from calling the store instead of searching for the information they need on the website.
  • Hide the discount code input field by displaying it only when the user clicks on "Do you have a discount code?" as it is done on mobile, to avoid distractions and/or checkout abandonment by customers who do not have a discount code.
